# Counters

Natural counter relationships between types — both emergent from gameplay and designed-in.

Each type lists candidate **Weaknesses** (what counters it) and **Resistances** (what it counters). Candidates are ranked by how grounded they are in real-world physics and lore to help prioritize which to formalize first.

## Grounding Scale

| Rating | Label | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| ★★★ | **Hard Physics** | Directly supported by real-world science — would be taught in a physics class |
| ★★☆ | **Soft Physics** | Based on real phenomena but requires some extrapolation or simplification |
| ★☆☆ | **Thematic** | Cultural, mythological, or game-lore based — intuitive but not strict science |

## Mutual Counters

Some relationships are dangerous for **both** types — these create interesting strategic tension.

| Type A | Type B | A's Angle | B's Angle |
|--------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Electric | Magnetic | Field disruption (changing E-fields disrupt B-fields) | Field containment (B-fields redirect current) |
| Sound | Earth | Seismic waves shatter stone | Dense stone blocks sound propagation |
| Sound | Magnetic | Vibration demagnetizes | EM fields could disrupt acoustic effects |
| Water | Electric | Water short-circuits | Electricity shocks through water |
| Air | Fire | Wind starves oxygen | Wind also fans flames |
| Poison | Plant | Toxins kill growth | Natural antidotes neutralize toxins |

## Design Principles

- Every type should have at least one natural counter
- No type should be uncounterable
- Counter relationships should feel intuitive (physics-based where possible)
- Counters should reduce effectiveness, not completely negate (avoid hard counters)
- The counter system should encourage diverse strategies, not create a rigid rock-paper-scissors
- Mutual counters add strategic depth — both players take risk engaging
