# Strategic Archetypes

## The Four Archetypes

Every type in the system aligns with one of four strategic archetypes that define its core approach to winning.

### Aggressive (Fire)
- **Tempo**: Fast-moving, burns quick
- **Goal**: Damage other players, destroy towers
- **Win Path**: Overwhelm opponents before they can build engines
- **Risk**: Burns through resources quickly; vulnerable if stalled

### Defensive (Earth)
- **Tempo**: Slow buildup of momentum
- **Goal**: Build up long-term engine through towers
- **Win Path**: Outlast opponents with superior infrastructure
- **Risk**: Slow to start; vulnerable to early aggression

### Flow (Water)
- **Tempo**: Quick flow of elements or spells
- **Goal**: Build up short-term options (spells, elements)
- **Win Path**: Outpace opponents with card/resource advantage
- **Risk**: Options without direction; can stall without a finisher

### Control (Air)
- **Tempo**: Reactive, counter-based
- **Goal**: Control other player actions through board control, card control, counters
- **Win Path**: Deny opponents their strategy while slowly advancing your own
- **Risk**: Requires reading opponents; weak when nothing to react to

Types appearing in multiple clusters create strategic flexibility:

| Type     | Clusters                              | Strategic Implication |

| Plant    | Generation, Protection                | Growth-oriented defense |
| Metal    | Protection, Aggressive                | Fortified offense |
