# Exploration: Light, Dark, and Time as Capstone Types

> **ID**: 007
> **Status**: Exploring
> **Date Opened**: 2026-03-22
> **Date Decided**: —
> **Related Docs**: [type-system-overview.md](../framework/type-system-overview.md), [augmenters.md](../mechanics/augmenters.md), [design-vision.md](../philosophy/design-vision.md), [balance-philosophy.md](../philosophy/balance-philosophy.md), [wizard-schools.md](../reference/scientists/wizard-schools.md), [006-multiplayer-economy-design.md](006-multiplayer-economy-design.md)

## Context

The current type system has 16 types in a clean hierarchy (4 primary → 4 secondary → 8 tertiary) plus 2 augmenter pairs (Light/Dark, Time/Space) floating outside the hierarchy with placeholder mechanics. This exploration asks: **what if Light and Dark aren't augmenters at all, but the final tier of the type system?**

The Einstein-Bohr debates — the most famous intellectual confrontation in science history — are about the nature of Light itself: wave or particle, deterministic or probabilistic, knowable or fundamentally uncertain. This maps perfectly to Light as the penultimate type: the point where all of physics converges into a single question about the nature of reality.

Meanwhile, Dark maps to the frontier where physics breaks down: dark energy, dark matter, black holes, the Planck scale, quantum gravity, the multiverse, the Big Bang, what's outside the universe. These are open problems — the edge of human knowledge. Dark is what we don't yet understand.

And if you master both Light AND Dark — the known and the unknown, the wave and the void — you reach **Time**: the ultimate type, the Theory of Everything, the final unification. Time is the one dimension that connects all of physics and all of human experience. Space, meanwhile, feels redundant with the Cosmic type (which already covers vastness, gravity, celestial mechanics).

This restructuring would change the type hierarchy from a flat augmenter model to a **pyramid with a capstone**:

```
                        TIME
                       /    \
                    LIGHT — DARK
                   /    \  /    \
              [8 Tertiary Types]
              /                  \
         [4 Secondary Types]
         /                  \
      [4 Primary Types]
```

### What This Replaces

Currently, augmenters are described as:
- **Light/Dark**: "Augmentations derived from the tertiary pairs" — vague, mostly TBD
- **Time/Space**: "Temporal and spatial manipulation mechanics" — vague, mostly TBD
- Both pairs sit outside the main hierarchy with no clear mechanical role

The new model:
- **Light**: 17th type. Capstone tier. Requires mastery of multiple primaries/secondaries to reach.
- **Dark**: 18th type. Capstone tier. Represents the frontier of unknowing.
- **Time**: 19th type (or meta-type). The ultimate. Requires both Light and Dark.
- **Space**: Folded into Cosmic (which already covers it). No longer a separate type.

## Part 1: Light — The Nature of Reality

### What Light Represents

Light is where all of physics converges. It's not just photons — it's the fundamental question of how reality works at its deepest level:

- **Wave-particle duality** — light is both and neither, the first crack in classical physics
- **The speed of light** — the universal speed limit, the constant that defines spacetime
- **E = mc²** — mass and energy are the same thing, connected through light
- **The photoelectric effect** — Einstein's Nobel Prize, proving light comes in quanta
- **Quantum electrodynamics** — the most precisely tested theory in physics, about light interacting with matter
- **Maxwell's equations** — unifying electricity, magnetism, and light into one framework

### How to Reach Light

Light shouldn't be easy. It's the convergence of multiple branches of knowledge. Candidates:

| Requirement | Thematic Justification | Mechanical Feel |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Any 2 primary types at high affinity** | Light emerges from mastering basic elements deeply | Specialist path — go deep in 2 areas |
| **Any 3 primary types** | Light requires broad elemental understanding | Generalist path — spread across the fundamentals |
| **All 4 primary types at any level** | Light unifies all states of matter | Completionist path — touch everything |
| **Any 2 secondary types** | Secondary mastery = enough sophistication for Light | Mid-game unlock — secondaries are already advanced |
| **1 primary + 1 secondary from different families** | Cross-disciplinary insight produces Light | Collab-friendly — encourages diverse building |
| **Specific type pair: Electric + Cosmic** | Maxwell (Electric→Magnetic) + Einstein (Light/Cosmic). Electromagnetism + relativity = light's nature | Narrow path — thematic but restrictive |

**Leaning**: Light requires **mastery of at least 2 different primary or secondary types**. The exact threshold depends on balance, but the principle is: Light comes from convergence, not from any single discipline.

### The Einstein-Bohr Debate as the Light Type's Identity

The Einstein-Bohr breakfast debates at the Solvay Conferences ARE this type's flavor:

- Einstein: "God does not play dice." Light is deterministic, knowable, a wave you can calculate.
- Bohr: "Stop telling God what to do." Light is probabilistic, uncertain, a quantum that defies classical logic.
- Einstein spent every morning presenting thought experiments to disprove quantum mechanics. Bohr spent every evening refuting them. Next morning, Einstein had another one.
- At the 1930 conference, Einstein presented the "photon box" — Bohr couldn't sleep, but overnight realized Einstein's own general relativity saved quantum mechanics. He used Einstein's theory against him.
- When Bohr died in 1962, the photon box was still drawn on his blackboard.

This debate is the **identity of Light**: the point where the greatest minds in history converged on the same question and couldn't resolve it. Light is both wave and particle. Both men were right. Both men were wrong. That's what makes it the capstone type.

### Who Heads the School of Light?

**Candidates**:

| Scientist | Claim to Light | Current Position in Game | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Albert Einstein** | Photoelectric effect (light = quanta), E=mc², special relativity (speed of light), general relativity (light bending in gravity) | Not currently in game | The obvious choice. His Nobel Prize was literally for explaining light. |
| **Niels Bohr** | Atomic model (electron energy levels = light emission), Copenhagen interpretation, complementarity | Not currently in game | The counter-position to Einstein. Together they define the debate. |
| **James Clerk Maxwell** | Unified electricity + magnetism + light | Currently: Magnetic Head | Already in the game. Moving him to Light Head would leave Magnetic without a head. |
| **Max Planck** | Planck constant, birth of quantum theory (blackbody radiation = quantized light) | Not in game | He started it all. Without Planck, neither Einstein nor Bohr has a foundation. |
| **Richard Feynman** | QED (quantum electrodynamics), the most precise theory of light ever | Not in game | Modern, charismatic, but maybe too recent for "head wizard" energy |

**Proposal**: **Einstein as Head of the School of Light**. Bohr as a rival or pupil. Planck as the other pupil (he laid the quantum foundation). The Einstein-Bohr tension IS the school's internal conflict — the wave-particle duality expressed as a philosophical feud between master and rival.

Possible school structure:
- **Head**: Albert Einstein — "School of Light" (wave-particle duality, the speed limit of reality)
- **Pupil A**: Max Planck — "School of the Quantum" (the smallest units of light, where classical physics breaks)
- **Pupil B**: Niels Bohr — "School of Complementarity" (both wave AND particle, the Copenhagen interpretation)
  - OR Bohr as a **rival** to Einstein rather than a pupil — their relationship was adversarial-but-respectful, not teacher-student

### Light Type Mechanics

As a capstone type, Light should feel powerful and earned:

- **Wildcard properties**: Light energy can substitute for any primary type (E = mc² — energy becomes anything). This was already proposed in the Splendor lens (decision 006).
- **Reveal/illuminate**: Light-type spells could reveal hidden information — see opponents' hands, peek at the deck, expose face-down cards. "Shedding light" on the unknown.
- **Speed/priority**: Light moves fastest. Light-type actions could grant initiative or bypass turn order. "At the speed of light."
- **Conversion**: Light converts between types. Spend Light to transform one type into another (nuclear transmutation, E=mc²).

## Part 2: Dark — The Frontier of the Unknown

### What Dark Represents

Dark is not evil. Dark is **the edge of human knowledge** — the places where physics currently breaks down, where the equations produce infinities, where we know something exists but can't explain it:

- **Dark matter** — 27% of the universe, never directly observed. We know it's there because galaxies shouldn't hold together without it. Fritz Zwicky first proposed it in 1933 (he's already in the game as Cosmic pupil B).
- **Dark energy** — 68% of the universe, completely unexplained. It's accelerating the expansion of everything. We have no idea what it is.
- **Black holes** — where spacetime curves to infinity and general relativity breaks down. The singularity at the center is where physics stops working.
- **The Planck scale** — 10⁻³⁵ meters. Below this, our models of space and time become meaningless. Quantum gravity lives here, and we don't have a theory for it.
- **The Big Bang singularity** — what happened "before" time? The question doesn't even make grammatical sense in physics.
- **Quantum gravity** — general relativity and quantum mechanics are both right, but they're incompatible. Reconciling them is the biggest open problem in physics.
- **The multiverse** — does our universe have siblings? Are the constants of physics different elsewhere? Is this the only reality?
- **The measurement problem** — why does observing a quantum system change it? What is "observation"? Nobody knows.

Dark is **the questions we can't yet answer**. It's the frontier. It's what keeps physicists up at night.

### Who Heads the School of Dark?

This is the school of the unknown — the scientists who pushed into territory where the equations break.

| Scientist | Claim to Dark | Current Position | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Stephen Hawking** | Black hole radiation, singularity theorems, popular science ambassador for the unknown | Not in game | The most famous physicist of the modern era. His work lives at the exact boundary where known physics breaks into the unknown. |
| **Werner Heisenberg** | Uncertainty principle — the fundamental unknowability of quantum states | Not in game | The principle that says SOME things cannot be known, even in theory. The mathematical proof that darkness is irreducible. |
| **Roger Penrose** | Singularity theorems (with Hawking), twistor theory, consciousness and physics | Not in game | Penrose diagrams literally map the boundary between known spacetime and singularities. |
| **Hugh Everett** | Many-worlds interpretation — every quantum measurement spawns parallel universes | Not in game | The multiverse. What's darker than infinite unseen realities? |
| **Fritz Zwicky** | Dark matter (first proposed) | Currently: Cosmic Pupil B ("School of the Void, The Seekers") | Already in the game. His school name — "The Seekers" of the invisible cosmos — is already perfectly Dark-coded. Could be promoted. |
| **John Wheeler** | Coined "black hole," "wormhole," "it from bit." Mentored Feynman and Everett. | Not in game | Named the darkness itself. |

**Proposal**: **Stephen Hawking as Head of the School of Dark**. His work on black hole radiation is literally about extracting information from the boundary of the unknowable. "A Brief History of Time" is the most widely read attempt to explain the darkness to the public.

Possible school structure:
- **Head**: Stephen Hawking — "School of Dark" (black holes, information paradox, the boundary of the knowable)
- **Pupil A**: Werner Heisenberg — "School of Uncertainty" (the principle that some things CANNOT be known)
- **Pupil B**: Hugh Everett — "School of Many Worlds" (the multiverse, branching realities, the darkness of parallel existence)

Fritz Zwicky could remain in Cosmic or be cross-referenced as a Dark ally — his "dark matter" discovery bridges both types.

### How to Reach Dark

Dark should be harder to reach than Light, or at least reached differently. If Light comes from convergence (mastering multiple known types), Dark comes from **pushing past the known**:

| Requirement | Thematic Justification | Mechanical Feel |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Mastery of at least 1 tertiary type** | Tertiary types are the "dark side" of their parents. Going deep into the dark side opens the door to true Dark. | Requires late-game investment in the advanced tier |
| **2 tertiary types from different families** | Cross-referencing two dark domains reveals the bigger darkness beneath | Harder, but thematically perfect — dark matter + dark energy = Dark |
| **Specific tertiary pair** (e.g., Cosmic + Ghost) | Cosmic = the void of space; Ghost = entropy and decay. Together they point toward the ultimate unknown. | Narrow path, strongly themed |
| **Any tertiary + any primary** | Combining advanced knowledge with fundamental elements creates the paradox that is Dark (we know so much yet understand so little) | Accessible but requires range |
| **Sacrifice/discard mechanic** | To gain Dark, you must give something up — discard cards, lose resources, embrace the unknown | Mechanically distinct from Light's convergence |

**Leaning**: Dark requires **at least 1 tertiary type at high affinity**, plus possibly a sacrifice or risk mechanic. Light is about mastering what you know. Dark is about confronting what you don't.

### Dark Type Mechanics

Dark should feel opposite to Light — where Light reveals and converts, Dark disrupts and destabilizes:

- **Disruption**: Dark-type spells interfere with opponents' engines. Force discards, disable instruments, break combos. Not destruction — *uncertainty*. "The Heisenberg Effect."
- **Hidden information**: Dark thrives in secrecy. Play Dark cards face-down. Dark effects resolve before being revealed. Opponents don't know what hit them.
- **Risk/reward amplification**: Dark doubles down. Dark-enhanced spells are more powerful but less predictable. Roll a die, flip a coin — Dark embraces the chaos that Einstein hated.
- **Void**: Dark can negate or absorb other types. A Dark counter doesn't just block — it erases. "Into the black hole."
- **The unknown payoff**: Some Dark cards have hidden VP values or effects revealed only at end-game. You don't know what they're worth until it's over — like dark energy, whose true nature remains unknown.

## Part 3: Time — The Theory of Everything

### What Time Represents

Time is the ultimate. If you master both Light (the nature of reality) and Dark (the frontier of the unknown), you've achieved the **Theory of Everything** — the unification of quantum mechanics and general relativity, the answer to every open question in physics.

Time is chosen over Space because:
- **Space is already covered by Cosmic** (gravity, celestial mechanics, expansion, Fritz Zwicky's dark matter, Hubble's galaxies)
- **Time is the one dimension that physics can't fully explain**. We can move freely in space but only forward in time. Why? Nobody knows.
- **Time connects everything**: thermodynamics (entropy = time's arrow), quantum mechanics (time evolution of wavefunctions), relativity (time dilation, spacetime), cosmology (the age of the universe, the Big Bang, the heat death)
- **The Theory of Everything would unify all of physics** — and the key unsolved problem is how time works at the quantum scale

### How to Reach Time

Time requires both Light AND Dark. You must understand the known AND the unknown.

| Requirement | Thematic Justification | Mechanical Feel |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Light + Dark affinity** | The Theory of Everything requires mastering both the known and the unknown | The ultimate late-game goal — you need BOTH capstones |
| **Light + Dark + a specific condition** | Maybe you also need a breakthrough, a certain number of Eureka moments, or to have collabed with every other player | Extra difficulty for extra reward |
| **Light + Dark + sacrifice** | The Theory of Everything costs everything — total commitment | You're going all-in on the final tier |

### Who Represents Time?

The Theory of Everything doesn't exist yet. No scientist has achieved it. This is the one school with no historical head wizard — or with a mythological/aspirational one.

| Candidate | Claim to Time | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **No one (empty throne)** | The Theory of Everything hasn't been discovered. The school exists, but no one has mastered it. The player who reaches Time IS the first. | Powerful thematic statement: you're making history |
| **Einstein (dual role)** | Einstein spent his final 30 years trying to unify physics. He failed. His unfinished quest IS Time. | Could head both Light and Time, but that's a lot of weight on one figure |
| **Stephen Hawking (dual role)** | Hawking's later work was explicitly aimed at the Theory of Everything. "A Brief History of Time" = literal title. | Same dual-role concern |
| **Emmy Noether** | Her theorem connects symmetries to conservation laws — the deepest structural insight in physics, underlying everything including time symmetry → energy conservation | Not in game. Her work is arguably the most fundamental theorem in all of physics. Under-recognized historically. |
| **The Player** | Reaching Time means YOU are the wizard who achieved what no one in history has. The card is blank until you play it. | The most "Discovery as Magic" option possible |

**Strongest option**: **The empty throne / The Player**. Time has no head wizard because the Theory of Everything doesn't exist yet. The school exists as a legend — the ultimate destination. If you reach it, you've done what Einstein, Hawking, Bohr, and everyone else couldn't. You ARE the head wizard.

Alternative: **Emmy Noether** as the keeper of Time. Her theorem is literally about the relationship between symmetries and conservation laws — time symmetry produces conservation of energy. She's arguably the most under-recognized genius in the history of physics. Making her the head of the ultimate type is a statement.

## Part 4: The Revised Hierarchy

### Before (Current)

```
Primary (4)  →  Secondary (4)  →  Tertiary (8)
                                    + Augmenters: Light/Dark, Time/Space (outside)
```

16 types + 4 vague augmenters = unclear structure

### After (Proposed)

```
Tier 1: PRIMARY (4)         Fire, Earth, Water, Air
            ↓ compose
Tier 2: SECONDARY (4)       Metal, Plant, Ice, Electric
            ↓ dark side
Tier 3: TERTIARY (8)        Radioactive, Cosmic, Poison, Sound,
                             Crystal, Ghost, Heat, Magnetic
            ↓ converge                    ↓ push beyond
Tier 4: LIGHT (1)                     DARK (1)
         mastery of the known           frontier of the unknown
                    ↓ unify ↓
Tier 5: TIME (1)
         the Theory of Everything
```

18 types total (16 + Light + Dark), with Time as the 19th and final.

### Space: Folded Into Cosmic

Space no longer needs to be a separate type because:
- **Cosmic** already covers: galaxies, gravity, expansion, dark matter (Zwicky), stellar physics
- **Cosmic's school** ("School of the Vast") is literally about the scale of space
- Space as a separate type always felt redundant — "what does a Space spell do that a Cosmic spell doesn't?"
- Spatial mechanics (board position, range, area effects) can be mechanics without being a type

If Space-themed mechanics are needed, they live under Cosmic. No separate Space type.

### How This Serves the Three Design Principles

| Principle | How Light/Dark/Time Serve It |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| **Discovery as Magic** | The capstone types ARE the ultimate discovery. Reaching Light feels like the eureka of eureka moments. Reaching Time feels like unlocking the secret of the universe. |
| **A World of Interaction** | Light requires convergence across multiple types. Dark requires deep tertiary investment. Time requires both. Every path to the capstone runs through interactions between other types. |
| **Progression & Advancement** | The hierarchy now has a clear summit. Primary → Secondary → Tertiary → Light/Dark → Time. The progression isn't just "more powerful" — it's "closer to understanding everything." |

### How This Connects to the Economy (Decision 006)

In the hybrid economy model (7 Wonders ages + Everdell triggers + Splendor tokens):

- **Light tokens as wildcards** still works — but now they're not just currency, they're evidence of mastery. You earn Light tokens by demonstrating convergent knowledge, not just by reserving cards.
- **Dark tokens as disruption** still works — earned through tertiary investment and risk-taking.
- **Time** could be the ultimate breakthrough — the one that triggers endgame, or the one that's worth massive VP, or the one that literally gives you an extra turn ("time manipulation").

## Part 5: The Center of the Diagram

### Visual Representation

The current type diagram is a wheel or web. With Light/Dark/Time, the center of that diagram finally has meaning:

```
              [Fire]————[Air]
             / |    \  /   | \
        [Metal] |  [Light]  | [Electric]
             \  |  /    \  |  /
          [Earth]—[TIME]—[Water]
             /  |  \    /  |  \
       [Crystal] | [Dark]   | [Ice]
             \  |    /  \   | /
             [Plant]————[Ice]
                  (tertiary ring around outside)
```

Or more precisely, a **layered mandala**:
- Outer ring: 8 tertiary types
- Middle ring: 4 secondary types
- Inner ring: 4 primary types
- Inner core: Light and Dark, orbiting each other
- Dead center: Time — the Theory of Everything

This gives the game a visual identity: the diagram itself is iconic, shareable, and tells the story of the type system at a glance. The center pulls you in. "What's at the center?" "That's Time. You have to master Light AND Dark to get there." "How do you get Light?" "You have to master multiple primary types." "...whoa."
