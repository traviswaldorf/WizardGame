# Decision: Towers vs. Pillars — Core Persistent Structure

> **ID**: 002
> **Status**: Exploring
> **Date Opened**: 2026-03-21
> **Date Decided**: —
> **Related Docs**: [glossary.md](../framework/glossary.md), [design-vision.md](../philosophy/design-vision.md), [discovery-experience.md](../philosophy/discovery-experience.md), [wizard-schools.md](../reference/scientists/wizard-schools.md)

## Context

Throughout the design docs, "towers" are the persistent structures players build — the primary asset that is built, protected, attacked, and destroyed. The term "tower" comes from early design and has propagated across type docs, mechanics, and spells.

There may be a better framing. The concept of **pillars** — as in pillars of research, pillars of knowledge — ties more naturally into the game's core fantasy of scientific discovery. A "pillar" could represent a player's investment in a branch of knowledge that yields energy and enables further discovery in that domain.

This decision affects terminology, theming, visual design, and potentially mechanics.

## Options

### Option A: Towers (Current)

**Description**: Keep the current "tower" terminology. Towers are structures you build on the board at element sources. They produce resources, can be attacked and defended, and are the primary win condition asset.

**Pros**:
- Already established across all design docs
- "Tower" is intuitive for a board game — physical structure on a board
- Clear metaphor for something that can be built, fortified, and destroyed
- Tower defense / tower building is a well-understood game genre

**Cons**:
- Doesn't connect to the scientific discovery theme
- "Tower" feels generic — could be any fantasy game
- No thematic link to knowledge, research, or progression
- Potential naming collision: "strategic pillars" (Aggressive, Defensive, Flow, Control) already use "pillar" for a different concept

**Interactions**: Works with Metal's shield/fortification identity. Works with Earth's defensive building identity. Neutral with the discovery experience.

---

### Option B: Pillars (Research Pillars)

**Description**: Rename "towers" to "pillars." A pillar represents a player's established research in a branch of elemental science. Pillars yield energy/discovery in their domain, enabling further progression. Building a pillar means you've invested enough to have a sustained presence in that element.

**Pros**:
- Directly ties into the scientific discovery theme — "pillars of research," "pillars of knowledge"
- Connects to the wizard school lore — each school rests on pillars of understanding
- "Building a pillar" feels like establishing a foundation of knowledge, not just placing a structure
- Thematic resonance: real science advances by building on previous work (standing on the shoulders of giants)
- "Destroying a pillar" means undermining someone's research — more thematically rich than knocking over a building

**Cons**:
- Naming collision with "strategic pillars" (Aggressive, Defensive, Flow, Control) — would need to rename one concept
- Less immediately visual than "tower" — harder to represent on a board
- May feel abstract for new players who don't yet understand the research metaphor
- Requires updating terminology across all existing docs

**Interactions**: Strong synergy with the discovery experience — pillars as research stations where you learn about elements. Connects to the wizard school concept — each school has pillars of knowledge. Creates a potential rename issue for the four strategic pillars (could become "archetypes," "philosophies," or "doctrines" instead).

---

### Option C: Hybrid — Stylized Towers as Research Centers

**Description**: Keep the word "tower" but reframe it visually and thematically as a research center / observatory / laboratory. A tower is where a wizard conducts research into an element — it's both a physical structure AND a seat of knowledge. Think of it as a wizard's tower where experiments happen and discoveries are made.

**Pros**:
- Keeps the intuitive "tower" game term that players already understand
- Adds thematic depth without changing terminology across all docs
- "Wizard's tower" is a well-known fantasy archetype — a place of study and power
- The visual can be stylized/abstract — a tower doesn't have to look like a castle turret, it could look like an observatory, a lab, a crystal spire
- A tower "yields energy" because research conducted there produces results
- No naming collision with strategic pillars

**Cons**:
- Less thematically distinctive than "pillar" — still just "tower" at the end of the day
- The research/discovery framing is visual flavor, not mechanical identity
- Doesn't change how players think about the concept as much as Option B would

**Interactions**: Works with existing doc terminology. Compatible with all current mechanics. Visual design can evolve independently of the game term.

---

### Option D: Something Else Entirely

Other terms to consider:

| Term | Metaphor | Feel |
|------|----------|------|
| **Foundation** | Foundational knowledge | Solid, but passive-sounding |
| **Obelisk** | Ancient monument of power | Mystical, but no science connection |
| **Beacon** | Source of energy/light | Active, broadcasting — fits discovery |
| **Nexus** | Connection point | Fits the "world of interaction" pillar |
| **Forge** | Place of creation | Strong for Metal, less universal |
| **Spire** | Reaching upward | Aspirational, visual, but generic |
| **Column** | Structural support | Similar to pillar, less evocative |
| **Monolith** | Mysterious structure | 2001: A Space Odyssey energy — discovery of the unknown |
| **Crucible** | Where elements are tested | Strong science/alchemy connection |
| **Apparatus** | Scientific instrument | Very on-theme but awkward in gameplay ("I attack your apparatus") |

---

## Notes

- **Naming collision resolved**: The four strategic pillars (Aggressive, Defensive, Flow, Control) have been renamed to **Strategic Archetypes** across all docs. "Pillar" is now free to use for the game structure if desired.
- The choice may differ between physical and digital versions — a board game might benefit from "tower" (tangible), while a digital version could lean into "pillar" or "crucible" (abstract).
- This decision should be informed by playtesting — which word do players naturally gravitate toward when describing the game?

## Recommendation

<!-- Current leaning, if any. Include reasoning. -->

Leaning toward exploring **Option B (Pillars)** for thematic resonance with the discovery/science identity, or **Option C (Stylized Towers)** for pragmatic simplicity. The naming collision with strategic archetypes has been resolved.
