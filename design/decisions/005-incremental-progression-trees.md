## Context

Exploring a game (or game mode) built around **incremental/idle game mechanics** layered onto the type system. Instead of a card/board game, this imagines the science-as-magic type system as the backbone of a progression game — think Cookie Clicker, Universal Paperclips, Kittens Game, or Realm Grinder, but where the progression skeleton IS the type hierarchy.

Each type has a **skill tree** you spend type energy to progress through. Deeper nodes unlock new abilities, automation, energy multipliers, instruments, and eventually access to secondary and tertiary types. The core loop: generate energy → invest energy → unlock things that generate more energy or open new branches.

**Genre inspirations**: Cookie Clicker (exponential scaling, prestige), Universal Paperclips (phase shifts that recontextualize the game), Kittens Game (resource complexity, multiple interlocking systems), Antimatter Dimensions (layered prestige), Realm Grinder (faction-based trees), Path of Exile (massive branching skill trees), Factorio (automation as progression).

---

## Core Loop

```
Generate type energy
       ↓
Spend energy on skill tree nodes
       ↓
Nodes unlock:
  - Passive generators (more energy/sec)
  - Multipliers (energy scales faster)
  - Discoveries (new mechanics, branch unlocks)
  - Instruments (tools that enable deeper progress)
  - Cross-type unlocks (secondary/tertiary access)
       ↓
More energy, new types, deeper trees
       ↓
Repeat at increasing scale
```

### What Makes It Incremental (Not Just a Tech Tree)

- **Numbers go up** — energy generation scales exponentially, not linearly
- **Automation layers** — early: you manually "cast" to generate energy. Later: passive generation, auto-casting, instruments that run themselves
- **Prestige/reset mechanics** — reset progress for permanent multipliers (see "Scientific Revolutions" below)
- **Phase shifts** — the game fundamentally changes character as you progress (gathering phase → building phase → automation phase → transcendence phase)
- **Idle component** — progress continues (slowly) when you're not actively playing

## Per-Type Skill Trees

Each primary type has a deep skill tree. You spend that type's energy to unlock nodes. Nodes branch, creating specialization paths within each type.

### What Nodes Unlock

| Node Type | Effect | Examples |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Generator** | Produces energy passively | "Candle" (+0.5 Fire/sec), "Furnace" (+5 Fire/sec) |
| **Multiplier** | Scales energy production | "Oxidation" (Fire generation ×1.5) |
| **Active Ability** | Click/cast for burst energy or effects | "Eruption" (instant 100 Fire) |
| **Discovery** | Unlocks new branch, mechanic, or knowledge | "Combustion Theory" (unlocks Furnace branch) |
| **Instrument** | Tool that enables deeper progress or cross-type effects | "Thermometer" (reveals Heat-related nodes) |
| **Gate** | Requires conditions beyond just energy | "Crucible" (requires Fire depth 5 + Earth depth 5 → opens Metal) |

### Example: Fire Skill Tree (Sketch)

```
[Spark] ← free starting node, manual click for +1 Fire
    |
[Candle] ← 10 Fire: +0.1 Fire/sec passive
    |
[Campfire] ← 50 Fire: +0.5 Fire/sec, unlocks "Tending" active
   / \
  /   \
[Forge]           [Torch]
200 Fire          150 Fire
+2 Fire/sec       Fire generation ×1.3
unlocks tools     illumination bonus
  |                    |
[Bellows]         [Bonfire]
500 Fire          400 Fire
+5 Fire/sec       +3 Fire/sec, area effect
req: Air depth 2  unlocks "Controlled Burn"
  |                    |
[Furnace]         [Wildfire]
2K Fire           1.5K Fire
+15 Fire/sec      Fire gen ×2 but consumes Earth
  |                    |
[Smelter]         [Combustion Engine]
10K Fire          8K Fire
req: Earth d5     DISCOVERY: unlocks thermodynamics branch
→ METAL GATE      → leads toward Heat/Radioactive
```

### Cross-Type Gates

Secondary types unlock when you reach sufficient depth in BOTH parent trees:

| Secondary | Gate Condition | What Opens |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Metal | Fire depth 5 + Earth depth 5 | Metal skill tree |
| Plant | Earth depth 5 + Water depth 5 | Plant skill tree |
| Ice | Air depth 5 + Water depth 5 | Ice skill tree |
| Electric | Fire depth 5 + Air depth 5 | Electric skill tree |

Secondary trees require secondary energy. But how do you get Metal energy before you have a Metal tree? **Bootstrap instruments** — the gate node itself produces a trickle of the secondary energy, enough to start the new tree.

Tertiary types are deeper still — potentially requiring secondary depth + specific instruments or discoveries.

## Discoveries

Discoveries are the "eureka moments" — named after real scientific breakthroughs, they don't just give you a number boost, they **change how the game works**.

### Example Discoveries (Fire Path)

| Discovery | Depth | Real Science | Game Effect |
|-----------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Controlled Fire** | 2 | Early humans | Unlocks passive Fire generation (before this, it's all manual clicking) |
| **Phlogiston Theory** | 4 | 17th c. chemistry | Fire multiplier, but it's "wrong" — later superseded by Combustion |
| **Combustion** | 7 | Lavoisier | Replaces Phlogiston with better multiplier. Unlocks Air+Fire synergy |
| **Thermodynamics** | 10 | Carnot, Kelvin | Unlocks Heat (tertiary) branch. Energy conversion between types |
| **Electromagnetic Radiation** | 14 | Maxwell | Fire + Electric synergy. Unlocks radiation mechanics |
| **Nuclear Fission** | 18 | Fermi, Meitner | Unlocks Radioactive (tertiary). Massive energy but requires containment |
| **Fusion** | 25 | Endgame | Combines Fire + Cosmic. Absurd energy generation |

### Superseded Discoveries

A fun mechanic borrowed from real science: **early discoveries get replaced by better ones.** Phlogiston Theory gives you a 2× multiplier at depth 4, but when you discover Combustion at depth 7, Phlogiston is "disproven" — it gets replaced with the superior Combustion model. This mirrors how science actually works and creates satisfying "paradigm shift" moments.

## Instruments

Instruments are persistent tools you build. They cost energy (sometimes from multiple types) and provide ongoing effects. They're the "buildings" of the incremental game but themed as scientific equipment.

### Example Instruments

| Instrument | Cost | Effect | Unlocks |
|------------|------|--------|---------|
| **Mortar & Pestle** | 100 Earth | +1 Earth/sec, basic material processing | Alchemy branch |
| **Bellows** | 200 Fire + 100 Air | +5 Fire/sec, requires Air investment | Cross-type synergy |
| **Crucible** | 500 Fire + 500 Earth | Bootstrap Metal energy (0.1 Metal/sec) | Metal skill tree |
| **Distillation Apparatus** | 300 Water + 200 Fire | Unlocks purification, +3 Water/sec | Poison branch |
| **Telescope** | 400 Air + 200 Earth | Reveals hidden nodes in trees | Cosmic branch |
| **Microscope** | 300 Water + 300 Earth | Reveals Plant/biological branches | Cellular biology discoveries |
| **Voltaic Pile** | 500 Metal + 300 Water | Bootstrap Electric energy | Electric skill tree |
| **Particle Accelerator** | 50K Electric + 20K Metal | Required for nuclear physics tier | Radioactive endgame |
| **CERN-scale Collider** | Endgame costs | Tertiary energy generation at scale | Prestige unlock? |

### Instrument Upgrades

Instruments can be upgraded — a Telescope becomes a Radio Telescope becomes a Space Telescope. Each tier costs more but reveals deeper/hidden content and provides larger bonuses.

## Scientific Revolutions (Prestige Mechanic)

The prestige/reset mechanic is themed as a **Scientific Revolution** — a paradigm shift that resets your progress but grants permanent bonuses based on how deep you got.

### How It Works

1. You progress through trees, building up energy generation
2. At some threshold, you can trigger a "Scientific Revolution"
3. All type energies reset to zero. All tree progress resets.
4. You gain **Paradigm Points** based on your total depth/progress
5. Paradigm Points provide permanent multipliers that persist across revolutions
6. Some instruments/discoveries may persist (or partially persist) as "fundamental knowledge"

### Revolution Tiers (Potential)

| Revolution | Threshold | Theme | Permanent Bonus |
|------------|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Classical** | Any primary at depth 10 | Greek natural philosophy | Base generation ×2 |
| **Enlightenment** | 2+ primaries at depth 15 | Scientific method | Insight generation, faster discovery |
| **Industrial** | Any secondary at depth 10 | Applied science | Automation mechanics unlock |
| **Modern** | 3+ secondaries unlocked | 20th century physics | Tertiary trees accessible |
| **Quantum** | Any tertiary at depth 10 | Quantum mechanics | <!-- TBD: endgame prestige --> |

### What Persists Across Revolutions

- Paradigm Points and their bonuses
- Discovery knowledge (you've "learned" Combustion — you don't forget it, you just need to rebuild the infrastructure)
- Instrument blueprints (rebuild faster, not from scratch)
- Some meta-currencies (Insight, Knowledge)

### What Resets

- All type energies
- Tree node progress (but unlock faster due to paradigm bonuses)
- Active generators and multipliers
- Instrument instances (but blueprints remain)

## The Science Narrative Arc

The progression through the game mirrors humanity's scientific journey:

```
Manual Experimentation (clicking, basic elements)
    ↓
Classical Elements (4 primaries understood)
    ↓
Alchemy & Early Chemistry (secondary types emerge)
    ↓
Scientific Method (first prestige — insight mechanics)
    ↓
Industrial Revolution (automation, instruments at scale)
    ↓
Modern Physics (tertiary types, energy/matter duality)
    ↓
Quantum/Nuclear (endgame, massive energy scales)
    ↓
Unified Theory? (final prestige? completion state?)
```

Each phase should feel meaningfully different to play — not just bigger numbers, but new mechanics, new decisions, new types of interaction.

## Wizard Schools as Faction Bonuses

The wizard school system could map to **faction choices** in this format (like Realm Grinder):

- Aligning with a wizard school gives bonuses to certain types and playstyles
- Schools could unlock unique instruments, discoveries, or tree branches
- You might choose a school at game start, or earn affiliation through your progression choices
- Different schools could favor different revolution strategies (rush one type deep vs. spread wide)

## Open Design Questions

- [ ] Single-type start or all-primaries-at-once? (Starting with just Fire and discovering the others vs. having all 4 primary trees from the start)
- [ ] How deep are trees? 20 nodes? 50? 100? (Depth affects pacing and the gap between types)
- [ ] Linear trees with branches, or web/graph structures?
- [ ] Can you respec tree choices, or are they permanent until prestige?
- [ ] How does multiplayer work, if at all? Competitive progression? Cooperative research?
- [ ] Is there an offline/idle component, or is it active-play only?
- [ ] How do augmenters (Time/Space, Light/Dark) fit? Meta-modifiers on the trees themselves?
- [ ] What's the endgame/completion state? Is there one, or is it infinite scaling?
- [ ] How many prestige layers? (Most successful incrementals have 2-3 prestige layers)
- [ ] Should discoveries have flavor text telling the real science story?
- [ ] Does the Phaser prototype direction still apply, or is this a different tech stack?
- [ ] How prominent is the "wizard" theming vs. straight science theming for this format?
- [ ] What about "failed experiments" — spending energy on a combination that doesn't work, but you learn something (negative results as a mechanic)?
