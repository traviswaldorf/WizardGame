# Game Name Ideas — Brainstorm

> **Status**: Exploration
> **Working title**: Wizard Game
> **Core concepts to capture**: A game that canonizes the tree of all human knowledge — how all physical things and forces connect. Wizard schools founded by real scientists. Elements, spells, and arcane knowledge grounded in real physics and chemistry. The feeling of ancient scholarly tradition meeting elemental mastery.

---

## Thematic Pillars for Naming

1. **Tree of Knowledge** — all human knowledge mapped into a unified system. Branches of science as branches of a literal tree.
2. **Book of Knowledge** — the arcane tome. Spell books, grimoires, scientific treatises as magical texts. The game itself is "the book."
3. **Old Scholarly Tradition** — real scientists as founding wizards. The game world treats physics papers as spell scrolls and labs as towers.
4. **Elemental Unity** — all forces, matter, and energy connected. The type system as a grand unified theory of magic.

---

## Style 1: Latin / Old Scholarly Papers

Modeled after real historical works like Newton's *Principia Mathematica*, Copernicus's *De Revolutionibus*, Gilbert's *De Magnete*. Latin evokes something old, mystical, but rooted in science.

| Name | Translation / Meaning | Evokes | Notes |
|------|----------------------|--------|-------|
| **Principia Elementorum** | "Principles of the Elements" | Newton's *Principia*. The foundational text of elemental science. | Strong parallel. The game IS the Principia of this world. |
| **Principia Magica** | "Principles of Magic" | The mathematical principles reimagined as magical principles. | More directly "magical." |
| **Principia Arcana** | "Principles of the Hidden/Secret" | Arcane = hidden knowledge. The secret principles underlying reality. | "Arcana" carries both tarot and D&D connotations — could be a plus or minus. |
| **Liber Elementorum** | "Book of the Elements" | A grimoire. The book that catalogs all elements. | "Liber" = book. Connects to "Book of Knowledge" concept. |
| **Liber Naturae** | "Book of Nature" | The idea that nature itself is a book to be read by the wise. | Historic concept — Galileo said "the book of nature is written in mathematics." |
| **Arbor Scientiae** | "Tree of Knowledge/Science" | Ramon Llull's 13th century work mapping all knowledge as a tree. | Directly captures the "Tree of Knowledge" concept. Historical precedent. |
| **Arbor Magna** | "The Great Tree" | A vast, branching tree of all knowledge. | Simpler, grander. The tree itself. |
| **De Rerum Natura** | "On the Nature of Things" | Lucretius's ancient poem explaining all natural phenomena through atoms and physics. | Already a famous title (55 BC). Could be too "taken." But the parallel is perfect — an ancient text explaining all of nature. |
| **Opus Magnum** | "The Great Work" | Alchemy's ultimate goal — transmuting base matter into gold, or achieving universal knowledge. | Short, punchy. "Magnum Opus" is the more common English order. |
| **Codex Elementis** | "Codex of the Elements" | An ancient codex. Scientific catalog meets spellbook. | "Codex" feels older and more artifact-like than "Liber." |
| **Scientia Arcanorum** | "Knowledge of Secrets" | The science of hidden things. | Bridges science and magic directly. |
| **Theatrum Mundi** | "Theater of the World" | Abraham Ortelius used this for his atlas. The world as a stage of elemental forces. | More theatrical/dramatic. |
| **Summa Elementorum** | "The Sum of Elements" | Modeled after Thomas Aquinas's *Summa Theologica*. A comprehensive treatise. | "Summa" implies completeness — the total, definitive work. |
| **Naturalis Historia** | "Natural History" | Pliny the Elder's encyclopedia of the natural world (77 AD). | Ancient encyclopedia energy. The game as a living catalog of nature. |

## Style 2: Book / Knowledge Themed

The game as a book of knowledge, grimoire, or scholarly text.

| Name | Meaning | Evokes | Notes |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| **The Book of Elements** | Direct, clear | A grimoire cataloging all elements and their interactions | Simple. Maybe too generic? |
| **The Grand Grimoire** | The great spellbook | Historical grimoire tradition. Power through written knowledge. | Existing real-world grimoire name — associations may help or hinder. |
| **Encyclopaedia Arcana** | Encyclopedia of the hidden | All arcane knowledge compiled. Encyclopedic scope. | Blends Enlightenment encyclopedias with arcane tradition. |
| **The Codex** | The book (singular, definitive) | There is only one book. This is it. | Short, punchy. But possibly too generic. |
| **Branches** | Branches of the tree of knowledge | Each element is a branch. Each school is a branch. | Abstract, modern. Short. |
| **The Unified Theory** | Physics concept — a theory of everything | Einstein's dream. All forces connected. | Very modern/scientific feel. Less mystical. |

## Style 3: Tree of Knowledge Themed

The game as a mapping of all knowledge, with elemental types as branches.

| Name | Meaning | Evokes | Notes |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| **Arbor** | Tree (Latin) | The tree of knowledge. Simple, ancient. | One word. Strong. But abstract — would people know it means "tree"? |
| **The Arboretum** | A collection of trees | A garden where all the branches of knowledge grow. | Scientific garden feel. |
| **Root and Branch** | From the base to the extremes | The full tree — primaries as roots, tertiaries as branches. | Idiomatic English phrase meaning "completely, thoroughly." |
| **The Canopy** | The top of the tree | The full spread of knowledge overhead. | Visual, but doesn't immediately say "game about elements." |
| **Dendron** | Tree (Greek) | Same concept, Greek root. Dendrite = branching. | More exotic sound. "Dendrite" also = neural branching. |

## Style 4: Wizard / School Themed

Leaning into the wizard school / scientist-as-wizard concept.

| Name | Meaning | Evokes | Notes |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| **The Schola** | The School (Latin) | Wizard academies. Houses of learning. | Short. Academic. |
| **Magisteria** | The Masteries / Domains of the Masters | Each element is a "magisterium" — a domain of mastery. | Grand, plural, suggests many schools. |
| **Philosophiae Naturalis** | "Of Natural Philosophy" | The old name for physics/science. Scientists were "natural philosophers." | Full title could be "Principia Philosophiae Naturalis" — but that's Newton's actual subtitle. |
| **The Academies** | Plural — many schools | Multiple wizard academies in tension and competition. | Modern English. Clear but plain. |
| **Collegium** | College / Guild (Latin) | A society of scholars/wizards. | Singular — feels like a shared institution rather than rival schools. |

## Style 5: Hybrid / Compound Names

Combining concepts.

| Name | Components | Feel | Notes |
|------|-----------|------|-------|
| **Principia Arboris** | Principia + Tree | "The Principles of the Tree" — the foundational laws of the knowledge tree | Combines scholarly gravitas with the tree concept |
| **Arbor Magica** | Tree + Magic | "The Magical Tree" — the tree of knowledge IS the magic system | Simple, clear, Latin |
| **Codex Naturae** | Codex + Nature | "The Codex of Nature" — an ancient artifact-book containing nature's laws | The game as a found artifact |
| **Liber Arborum** | Book + Trees | "Book of Trees" — the book that maps all branches of knowledge | Direct combo of Book + Tree concepts |
| **Opus Elementis** | Great Work + Elements | "The Elemental Work" — the alchemical great work, but for elements | Alchemy meets element system |
| **Scientia Naturalis** | Knowledge + Nature | "Natural Science" — what the wizards actually practice | Clean, direct. "Scientists" = "natural knowers" |
