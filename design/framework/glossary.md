# Glossary

## Core Concepts

**Type** — An elemental classification that defines a card, spell, or entity's domain. Types determine interactions, synergies, and counters.

**Tier** — The level of a type within the hierarchy: Primary (base), Secondary (combination), or Tertiary (dark/energy pair).

**Dark Pair** — The tertiary type that represents the deeper physics or energy side of a primary or secondary type (e.g., Radioactive is the dark pair of Fire).

**Old Name** — The lore-based name for a type, using a thematic -o postfix convention (e.g., Pyro for Fire, Cosmo for Cosmic).

**Archetype** — One of four strategic archetypes that define how a type wants to win: Aggressive, Defensive, Flow, or Control.

**Cluster** — A cross-cutting grouping of 3 types that share a thematic play pattern (Generation, Control, Protection, Aggressive).

## Gameplay Terms

**Tower** — A persistent structure a player builds. Towers are the primary asset — built, protected, attacked, and destroyed.

**Element** — A resource gathered and spent to cast spells. Elements correspond to the type system.

**Spell** — An action card played by spending elements. Spells have types, costs, effects, and may have augments/triggers.

**Storm** — A persistent, board-wide environmental effect tied to a type. Storms change the game conditions for ALL players but asymmetrically favor the triggering type. Each type has a Positive Storm (benefits your playstyle) and a Negative Storm (creates adverse conditions). Storms persist for multiple turns, feel like weather, and are distinct from spells or super powers. See [storms.md](../mechanics/storms.md).

**Super Power** — A major personal ability or ultimate tied to a type. Unlike Storms, Super Powers are targeted (not board-wide), instant or very short duration, and intentionally activated by the player. They represent "I made a big play" moments. Each type has 3 candidate super powers under consideration.

**Design Decision** — An unresolved game mechanic or feature choice captured in a structured decision doc under `design/decisions/`. Decisions track options, pros/cons, playtest notes, and final rationale. See [decisions/](../decisions/).

**Status Effect** — A condition applied to a player, tower, or element source that persists for a duration (e.g., Burn, Freeze, Paralyze).

**Permanent Effect** — A spell or ability whose effect persists on the board beyond the turn it was played.

## Spell Mechanics

**Archetype** — The mechanical category of a spell's effect: Damage, Destroy, Counter, Steal, Draw, Revive, Discard, Discount, Prevent, Block.

**Augment** — A modifier that can be applied to a spell to change or amplify its effect, usually at an additional cost (e.g., "Discard a card to do extra damage").

**Trigger** — A condition that causes a spell effect to activate (e.g., "When played", "When activated", "At end of turn").

**Combo** — A spell or effect that requires or benefits from combining two different types.

**Equation** — A reaction formula where combining types or effects produces a new type (e.g., Fire + Poison = Radioactive).

**Synergy** — A beneficial interaction between two types when used together.

**Counter** — A type relationship where one type naturally opposes or weakens another.

## Strategic Categories

**Aggro** — Spell archetypes focused on dealing damage and destroying: Damage, Destroy.

**Control** — Spell archetypes focused on disruption and denial: Counter, Steal.

**Search** — Spell archetypes focused on resource acquisition: Draw.

**Growth** — Spell archetypes focused on building advantage: Revive, Discount.

**Defensive** — Spell archetypes focused on protection: Prevent, Block.

## Augmenter Terms

**Augmenter** — A type pair (Time/Space, Light/Dark) that exists outside the main 8-type system and modifies or enhances other types.

**Light Form** — The standard expression of a primary/secondary type.

**Dark Form** — The tertiary pair expression, representing the shadow or deeper energy side (e.g., Poison is the Dark form of Water).
