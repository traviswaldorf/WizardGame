# Wizard Schools — Historical Figures as Elemental Founders

> **Status**: Exploration
> **Concept**: Each elemental type is a "school" of wizardry, led by a historical scientist depicted as the founding wizard of that tradition. Each school has two pupil sub-branches representing contrasting schools of thought within the domain.
> **Flavor direction**: These are real scientists reimagined through an alternate lens — physics/chemistry/math "wizards" who founded branches of elemental mastery. Art would depict them in wizard robes, in labs-as-towers, with their instruments as magical artifacts.

---

## Primary Types

### Fire — School of Combustion

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Antoine-Laurent Lavoisier** (1743–1794) | Founded modern chemistry; identified oxygen's role in combustion, disproving phlogiston theory | He explained what fire *is* |
| Pupil A | **James Prescott Joule** (1818–1889) | Demonstrated the mechanical equivalent of heat — that work and heat are interchangeable forms of energy | **School of Mechanical Energy** — fire as measurable, convertible energy. The furnace. |
| Pupil B | **Joseph von Fraunhofer** (1787–1826) | Discovered dark absorption lines in the solar spectrum, founding solar spectroscopy | **School of Solar Fire** — fire as stellar/cosmic phenomenon. The star. |

**Tension**: Terrestrial combustion (Joule) vs. celestial fire (Fraunhofer). The furnace vs. the star.

### Earth — School of Mass

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Isaac Newton** (1643–1727) | Formulated universal gravitation and laws of motion | He defined gravity, mass, and force |
| Pupil A | **James Hutton** (1726–1797) | Proposed uniformitarianism — Earth shaped by slow, continuous geological processes over immense time | **School of Deep Time** — Earth as ancient, patient, enduring. Slow buildup. |
| Pupil B | **Alfred Wegener** (1880–1930) | Proposed continental drift — continents were once joined and have moved apart | **School of Shifting Ground** — Earth as dynamic, mobile. The ground itself moves. |

**Tension**: Permanence and slow continuity (Hutton) vs. upheaval and continental motion (Wegener). Stability vs. mobility.

---

### Water — School of Fluids

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Archimedes of Syracuse** (c. 287–212 BC) | Discovered buoyancy (Archimedes' Principle); pioneered hydrostatics and volume measurement by displacement | The original scientist of water — "Eureka!" |
| Pupil A | **Daniel Bernoulli** (1700–1782) | Formulated Bernoulli's Principle — fluid speed relates inversely to pressure | **School of the Current** — water in motion. Flow, rivers, dynamics. |
| Pupil B | **Blaise Pascal** (1623–1662) | Formulated Pascal's Law — pressure in a confined fluid transmits equally in all directions | **School of the Deep** — water under pressure. Hydraulics, the crushing weight of depth. |

**Tension**: Flowing water (Bernoulli) vs. still, pressurized water (Pascal). The river vs. the ocean deep. Dynamics vs. statics.

---

### Air — School of Pressure

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Evangelista Torricelli** (1608–1647) | Invented the barometer; proved the atmosphere has weight and exerts pressure | He made the invisible visible |
| Pupil A | **Robert Boyle** (1627–1691) | Formulated Boyle's Law — gas pressure and volume are inversely proportional | **School of Compression** — air as controllable, compressible. Pneumatics, contained gas. |
| Pupil B | **Gaspard-Gustave de Coriolis** (1792–1843) | Described the Coriolis effect — deflection of air masses due to Earth's rotation, governing weather patterns | **School of the Wind** — air as free, planetary-scale. Cyclones, atmospheric circulation. |

**Tension**: Confined, controlled gas (Boyle) vs. free, planetary-scale wind (Coriolis). The lab vs. the sky.

## Secondary Types

### Metal — School of Hardness

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Friedrich Mohs** (1773–1839) | Created the Mohs hardness scale — first systematic classification of material hardness | He gave us the language of hardness |
| Pupil A | **Henry Bessemer** (1813–1898) | Invented the Bessemer process — first inexpensive method for mass-producing steel | **School of the Forge** — metal transformed by fire and force. Industrial shaping. The blacksmith. |
| Pupil B | **William Lawrence Bragg** (1890–1971) | Pioneered X-ray crystallography (Bragg's Law), revealing atomic structure of metals | **School of Structure** — metal understood through its hidden atomic architecture. The crystallographer. |

**Tension**: Shaping metal through brute force (Bessemer) vs. understanding metal through its atomic structure (Bragg). Making vs. knowing.

---

### Plant — School of Growth

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Charles Darwin** (1809–1882) | Formulated evolution by natural selection — how all living things grow, adapt, and diversify | The central figure of biology and life |
| Pupil A | **Gregor Mendel** (1822–1884) | Discovered laws of heredity through pea plant experiments, founding genetics | **School of the Seed** — life as encoded information. The blueprint inside the seed. Internal code. |
| Pupil B | **Thomas Robert Malthus** (1766–1834) | Proposed populations grow exponentially while resources grow linearly | **School of Overgrowth** — life as unchecked growth and competition. Doubling time, population pressure. |

**Tension**: *What* grows (Mendel — the internal code of heredity) vs. *how fast* it grows (Malthus — exponential math of proliferation). Blueprint vs. explosion. Code vs. chaos. Malthus directly inspired Darwin's natural selection concept.

---

### Ice — School of Cold

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **William Thomson, Lord Kelvin** (1824–1907) | Established the absolute temperature scale and the concept of absolute zero | He defined the ultimate limit of cold |
| Pupil A | **Heike Kamerlingh Onnes** (1853–1926) | First to liquefy helium; discovered superconductivity at extremely low temperatures | **School of Absolute Cold** — pursuing absolute zero. Cryogenics, exotic quantum behaviors at extreme cold. |
| Pupil B | **Josiah Willard Gibbs** (1839–1903) | Founded chemical thermodynamics and the theory of phase transitions | **School of Phase Change** — the boundaries between states of matter. When and how matter transforms. |

**Tension**: Pushing toward the extreme destination (Onnes — colder, colder, absolute zero) vs. studying the transitions themselves (Gibbs — the moment water becomes ice). Destination vs. journey.

---

### Electric — School of Current

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Michael Faraday** (1791–1867) | Discovered electromagnetic induction; invented the electric motor and generator | He made electricity *usable* |
| Pupil A | **Alessandro Volta** (1745–1827) | Invented the voltaic pile (first battery) — electricity generated chemically and stored | **School of Stored Lightning** — electricity as potential. Contained, patient, coiled power. Voltage. |
| Pupil B | **Andre-Marie Ampere** (1775–1836) | Founded electrodynamics; defined the nature of electric current | **School of the Living Current** — electricity as flow. Moving charges, dynamic streaming power. Amperage. |

**Tension**: Stored potential (Volta — the battery on the shelf) vs. flowing current (Ampere — lightning coursing through wire). Potential vs. flow.

## Tertiary Types

### Radioactive — School of the Atom

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Marie Curie** (1867–1934) | Discovered radium and polonium; coined "radioactivity"; first person to win Nobels in two sciences. Died from radiation exposure. | She IS radioactivity — her life and death inseparable from it |
| Pupil A | **Lise Meitner** (1878–1968) | First to theoretically explain nuclear fission — the splitting of heavy atomic nuclei | **School of Fission (The Splitters)** — power through division. Chain reactions, bombs, reactors. Breaking apart. |
| Pupil B | **Hans Bethe** (1906–2005) | Explained nuclear reactions powering the Sun — hydrogen fusing into helium | **School of Fusion (The Fusionists)** — power through union. Stellar energy, combining light atoms. Joining together. |

**Tension**: Splitting apart (Meitner — fission, the bomb) vs. joining together (Bethe — fusion, the star). Destruction vs. creation. Perhaps the most elegant tension in the roster.

---

### Cosmic — School of the Vast

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Edwin Hubble** (1889–1953) | Proved galaxies exist beyond the Milky Way; discovered the universe is expanding | He revealed the true size of the cosmos |
| Pupil A | **Henrietta Swan Leavitt** (1868–1921) | Discovered period-luminosity relationship of Cepheid stars — first reliable cosmic distance measurement | **School of the Light (The Measurers)** — the visible, measurable cosmos. Using starlight as a ruler. |
| Pupil B | **Fritz Zwicky** (1898–1974) | First to propose dark matter — galaxy clusters contain far more mass than visible matter | **School of the Void (The Seekers)** — the invisible, hidden cosmos. Dark matter, unseen mass. |

**Tension**: The visible universe (Leavitt — what we can see and measure) vs. the invisible universe (Zwicky — dark matter, what we cannot). Light vs. dark. Connects to the Light/Dark augmenter pair.

### Poison — School of Toxicology

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Paracelsus** (Theophrastus von Hohenheim, 1493–1541) | Established toxicology; "the dose makes the poison" — the LD50 principle | The father of toxicology. Renaissance alchemist — the most ancient-feeling Head Wizard. |
| Pupil A | **Mathieu Orfila** (1787–1853) | Founded forensic toxicology — systematic methods to detect poisons in the body | **School of Detection (The Revealers)** — identifying and understanding poison. The detective. Finding the antidote. |
| Pupil B | **Carl Wilhelm Scheele** (1742–1786) | Discovered chlorine, hydrogen cyanide, hydrogen fluoride, and numerous toxic compounds. Likely died from exposure to his own discoveries. | **School of Corrosion (The Synthesizers)** — creating and concentrating poison. The alchemist who makes it. |

**Tension**: Detecting poison (Orfila — forensics, antidotes, defense) vs. creating poison (Scheele — synthesis, concentration, offense). Finding vs. making.

### Sound — School of Acoustics

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Alexander Graham Bell** (1847–1922) | Invented the telephone; pioneered acoustics and sound transmission. Decibel named after him. | He made sound travel |
| Pupil A | **Ernst Chladni** (1756–1827) | Visualized sound vibrations as geometric patterns on vibrating plates (Chladni figures) | **School of Resonance (The Pattern-Makers)** — sound as structure and geometry. Standing waves, hidden order in vibration. |
| Pupil B | **Christian Doppler** (1803–1853) | Described the Doppler effect — frequency changes based on relative motion | **School of the Wave (The Motion-Readers)** — sound as motion and change. Dynamic, relative, shifting. |

**Tension**: Static resonance (Chladni — sound standing still, geometric patterns) vs. dynamic waves (Doppler — sound in motion, shifting with the observer). The standing wave vs. the moving wave.

### Crystal — School of Frequency

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Heinrich Hertz** (1857–1894) | First to prove electromagnetic waves exist; measured their frequency. Hz named after him. | He proved invisible waves vibrate at measurable frequencies |
| Pupil A | **Pierre Curie** (1859–1906) | Discovered piezoelectricity — certain crystals generate electric charge under mechanical stress | **School of the Living Crystal (Piezoelectrics)** — crystals that vibrate, generate electricity, keep time. Active, responsive. The clock. |
| Pupil B | **Auguste Bravais** (1811–1863) | Classified all possible crystal lattice structures into 14 arrangements (Bravais lattices) | **School of the Eternal Lattice (Crystallographers)** — perfect repeating geometry. Unchanging, mathematical, timeless. The gem. |

**Tension**: The vibrating crystal (Pierre Curie — oscillation, energy conversion, time-keeping) vs. the still lattice (Bravais — perfect static geometry). Dynamic frequency vs. static structure. The clock vs. the gem.

**Note**: Pierre Curie is Marie Curie's husband. In game lore, the Curie family spans two wizard schools (Crystal and Radioactive).

### Ghost — School of Entropy

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Ludwig Boltzmann** (1844–1906) | Founded statistical mechanics; gave entropy its mathematical definition (S = k log W). Died by suicide, partly from rejection of his ideas. | He explained *why things die* — why order decays into chaos |
| Pupil A | **Ernest Rutherford** (1871–1937) | Discovered the concept of radioactive half-life and laws of radioactive decay | **School of Decay (The Reapers)** — specific things dying on specific timescales. Atoms transmuting, matter crumbling. |
| Pupil B | **Rudolf Clausius** (1822–1888) | Formulated the second law of thermodynamics; coined "entropy" — the universe tends toward maximum disorder | **School of Entropy (The Doomsayers)** — universal, cosmic death. All energy dissipates. Heat death of the universe. |

**Tension**: Local decay (Rutherford — this atom, this half-life, measurable rates) vs. cosmic entropy (Clausius — everything dies eventually, the universal arrow of time). The particular ghost vs. the universal one.

### Heat — School of Thermodynamics

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **Sadi Carnot** (1796–1832) | Founded thermodynamics; established maximum efficiency of heat engines (Carnot cycle) | He defined the rules of heat — how much work you can extract from it |
| Pupil A | **Count Rumford** (Benjamin Thompson, 1753–1814) | Proved heat is generated by mechanical work (friction), overthrowing the caloric theory | **School of Friction (The Converters)** — heat created by action. Rubbing, drilling, braking. Heat is a process. |
| Pupil B | **Joseph Fourier** (1768–1830) | Developed Fourier's Law of heat conduction; mathematically described how heat flows through materials | **School of Conduction (The Spreaders)** — heat transferred and spreading. Flow from hot to cold. Heat moves. |

**Tension**: How heat is *created* (Rumford — friction, conversion) vs. how heat *spreads* (Fourier — conduction, transfer). Generation vs. propagation. The spark vs. the glow.

### Magnetic — School of Fields

| Role | Name | Contribution | School of Thought |
|------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Head Wizard** | **James Clerk Maxwell** (1831–1879) | Unified electricity, magnetism, and light into Maxwell's equations | The grand theorist of invisible fields and flux |
| Pupil A | **Nikola Tesla** (1856–1943) | Invented AC motor, Tesla coil; pioneered rotating magnetic fields and wireless energy | **School of the Field (The Broadcasters)** — magnetism as active, radiant, far-reaching. Transmitting power wirelessly. Reaching out. |
| Pupil B | **William Gilbert** (1544–1603) | Published *De Magnete* (1600) — first systematic study of magnetism; proposed Earth is a giant magnet | **School of the Lodestone (The Attractors)** — magnetism as inherent, attracting, grounding. The compass, the pull inward. |

**Tension**: Broadcasting outward (Tesla — rotating fields, wireless transmission) vs. attracting inward (Gilbert — lodestones, compass needles, the Earth's pull). Emission vs. attraction.

## Cross-School Connections

Notable relationships between wizard schools:

| Connection | Note |
|-----------|------|
| **The Curie Family** | Marie Curie leads Radioactive; Pierre Curie is a pupil under Crystal. Husband and wife across two schools. |
| **Malthus → Darwin** | Malthus (Plant pupil) directly inspired Darwin (Plant head wizard). Intellectual lineage within the school. |
| **Leavitt → Hubble** | Leavitt (Cosmic pupil) gave Hubble (Cosmic head wizard) the tool to measure the universe. She enabled him. |
| **Paracelsus the Ancient** | The oldest head wizard (1493). Poison's school feels arcane and alchemical compared to the others. |
| **Boltzmann's Tragedy** | Ghost's head wizard died from despair over rejection of his ideas. Life consumed by entropy — his own school's domain. |
| **Scheele's Death** | Poison pupil B likely died from his own toxic discoveries. The synthesizer poisoned by his synthesis. |
