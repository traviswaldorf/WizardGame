# Elden Ring / Dark Souls — Relevance to Wizard Game

> **Primary Influence**: Progressive map expansion, minimal hand-holding, community discovery culture, New Game+

## Map Expansion and Scale Revelation

Elden Ring's parchment map system:
- **Map fragments must be found physically**: Each region's map is blank until you travel to a stele and interact with it
- **The blank map is still useful**: Major roads and elevation contours faintly visible — enough to navigate, not to spoil. Communicates "there is structure here" without giving it away
- **Underground areas are completely absent from the surface map**: Siofra River, Nokron, Ainsel River, Deeproot Depths — entire parallel maps underneath. Discovery requires finding unremarkable entrances (a well, a ruin, a cave)
- **The descent**: You step on an elevator in a small building. It takes you down, and down, and down. You arrive in a vast underground sky filled with stars and ancient architecture. No warning about what was below. Deliberate visual shock.
- **The underground has its own blank map**: Even after finding it, you must discover a second map fragment system. A world within the world.

**Takeaway**: Progressive revelation of *scale*. The game is much bigger than you thought. Our Tier 1 → Tier 2 transition should produce this feeling — "there's so much more than I realized."

## Community Culture

FromSoftware games have developed a culture directly relevant to our "Fight Club" principle:

- **"Don't spoil, just point"**: Community values helping players get the *experience* of discovery, not the fact of it
- **Illusory walls**: Hidden doors discovered by hitting walls. Never marked. The habit of searching becomes rewarding in itself
- **Ambiguous message system**: Players leave short template-constrained messages. Can't write explicit spoilers — forced ambiguity preserves discovery while enabling hints
- **First playthrough as sacred rite**: Spoiling a boss or major secret is treated as a genuine social transgression
- **Discovery as social currency**: "I found X" is a genuine achievement, not a checklist item

## New Game Plus (NG+)

- **Enemies scale up +50-100% per cycle** (caps around NG+7)
- **World layout does not change**: Same shortcuts, same paths. Navigating a *known* space at higher difficulty is a different skill than initial discovery
- **New items appear only in NG+**: Mechanical reason to replay beyond challenge
- **Boss fights change subtly**: Altered movesets, additional enemies. Enough to punish muscle-memory complacency

**Takeaway**: NG+ reframes the same system as a harder puzzle. Discovery phase is over; mastery phase begins. This models our Tier 2 (meta-game) and Tier 3 (re-discovery).

## Information Withholding as Design Tool

- **Item descriptions carry all lore**: Written in-universe, not as instruction. "The blade is imbued with a special magic that is released with a strong attack" — one sentence teaches a mechanic as flavor text
- **NPC dialogue is oblique**: "I did find a most extraordinary sight... deep below the earth, if you care to look." A hint, not a waypoint
- **Stats are unexplained**: "Poise," "Discovery," "Adaptability" — no tooltips. Players experiment, research, or ask
- **Enemies as gates**: A powerful enemy in an early area communicates "come back later" without a level-lock screen

## Elemental Resistance System

- **Damage types**: Physical (Standard, Strike, Slash, Pierce) + Elemental (Fire, Lightning, Holy, Magic)
- **Flat absorption per type**: A dragon absorbs 90% fire but 0% lightning. Not displayed — discovered through experimentation
- **Status buildup/proc system**: Each hit fills a hidden meter (Bleed, Poison, Frostbite, etc.). When full, catastrophic proc fires. Rapid attacks disproportionately effective. Creates dramatic threshold moments.
- **None of this is displayed during gameplay**: Finding that lightning destroys the dragon IS a discovery moment

**Takeaway**: The buildup/proc system is highly relevant for our status effects. "A little of this type" = negligible. "Enough of this type" = catastrophic threshold. More dramatic than linear resistance math.

## Direct Application to Our Game

| FromSoft Mechanic | Our Translation |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Blank map with visible outline | Show the shape of the type system (8 elements, they interact) without revealing what interactions do |
| Progressive scale revelation | Tier 1 discovers types. Tier 2 reveals the depth of strategy. Tier 3 is a whole new world |
| Community spoiler culture | The "Fight Club" principle — natural protection of first-time experience |
| Buildup/proc status effects | Status effects that build over multiple applications before triggering — dramatic thresholds |
| Deliberate opacity | Don't list type advantages on cards. Let players discover through play |
| NG+ as mastery phase | Tier 2 meta-game uses same system, deeper strategy. Tier 3 resets the discovery |
